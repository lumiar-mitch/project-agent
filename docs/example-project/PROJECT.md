# Riverton City Council — Website Replacement

> This is a **fictional worked example** shipped with Project Agent. It shows what a healthy
> instance looks like — good registers, a clean meeting synthesis, and a generated RAID view. Use
> it for demos and as a reference. It is not a real project.

## In one paragraph

Riverton City Council is replacing its ageing public website (last rebuilt in 2016) with a modern,
accessible, mobile-first platform. The current site fails WCAG 2.1 AA in several places, its CMS is
out of vendor support, and residents cannot complete common tasks — reporting a pothole, paying
rates, booking a facility — online. The project delivers a new CMS, a redesigned information
architecture, and online forms for the top 10 resident tasks, migrating roughly 400 priority pages.
Delivery partner is Northbank Digital. Target go-live is 30 November 2026.

## Scope

**In scope**
- New CMS on the council's approved cloud tenancy
- Redesigned information architecture and top-tasks navigation
- Online forms for the 10 highest-volume resident requests
- Migration of ~400 priority pages (of ~2,100 total; the long tail is archived, not migrated)
- WCAG 2.1 AA accessibility compliance
- Content author training for 25 staff

**Out of scope**
- Integration with the finance/rates back-end (deferred to a later phase; forms hand off by email
  and reference number for now)
- The intranet
- Migrating the archived long-tail pages
